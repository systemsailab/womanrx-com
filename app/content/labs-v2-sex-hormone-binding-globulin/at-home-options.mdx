# SHBG (Extended) Test: At-Home and Finger-Prick Options, Normal Ranges, and What Your Results Mean

<TLDR>
Sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG) controls how much testosterone and estrogen your body can actually use. An "extended" panel pairs SHBG with total testosterone and estradiol to calculate free hormone levels. At-home finger-prick options now make this panel accessible without a clinic visit, and results mean very different things depending on your life stage.
</TLDR>

<AtAGlance>
- Test type / SHBG (extended): SHBG + total testosterone + estradiol, with calculated free testosterone and free estrogen index
- Normal SHBG range (reproductive years) / 18-144 nmol/L (varies by lab and life stage)
- At-home options available / Yes, finger-prick dried blood spot and venous self-collection kits
- Turnaround time / typically 3-7 business days from sample receipt
- Life-stage note / SHBG rises sharply in pregnancy and with oral estrogen; it falls in PCOS and after menopause
- PCOS relevance / Low SHBG raises free androgen index and drives hyperandrogenism symptoms even when total testosterone looks normal
- Key calculation / Free Androgen Index (FAI) = (Total T nmol/L / SHBG nmol/L) x 100
- Fasting required / Not strictly required, but consistent timing reduces variability
- Cycle timing / Best drawn on days 2-4 of the menstrual cycle for a hormonal baseline in premenopausal women
</AtAGlance>

## What Is SHBG and Why Does the "Extended" Panel Matter?

SHBG is a glycoprotein produced mainly in the liver that binds tightly to testosterone, dihydrotestosterone (DHT), and, to a lesser degree, estradiol. Only the hormone fraction that is NOT bound to SHBG, meaning the free or albumin-bound portion, can enter cells and produce biological effects. Total testosterone can sit in a "normal" reference range while free testosterone is very low (or very high), entirely depending on how much SHBG is present.

The standard SHBG test measures only SHBG. The extended panel adds [total testosterone and estradiol](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17942849/), allowing calculation of:

- **Free Androgen Index (FAI):** (Total T nmol/L / SHBG nmol/L) x 100. An FAI above 4-5 in women suggests excess free androgen activity, even when total T is not flagged.
- **Free Estrogen Index (FEI):** (Total E2 nmol/L / SHBG nmol/L) x 100, used less commonly but relevant in perimenopausal monitoring.
- **Calculated free testosterone:** using the Vermeulen equation, which is the most widely validated formula for women per [The Endocrine Society's 2023 clinical practice guideline on testosterone therapy](https://academic.oup.com/jcem/article/104/10/4660/5556834).

Without the full extended panel, you are seeing only part of the picture. A woman with a total testosterone of 40 ng/dL and SHBG of 20 nmol/L has dramatically more free testosterone than a woman with the same total T and SHBG of 100 nmol/L.

### Why SHBG Is Not Just a "Testosterone Test"

SHBG affects estrogen availability too. In postmenopausal women on oral estradiol, the liver first-pass effect raises SHBG significantly, which then binds a portion of the estradiol back and may blunt symptom relief. Transdermal estradiol does not raise SHBG to the same degree, which is one pharmacokinetic reason some clinicians prefer the transdermal route for women with already-high SHBG. This distinction is [supported by data from the KEEPS trial](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22977988/) and multiple smaller pharmacokinetic studies.

### What Raises and Lowers SHBG

| Factor | Direction | Clinical Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Oral estrogen (pills, oral HRT) | Raises SHBG | Can suppress free T and free E |
| Pregnancy | Raises SHBG | Can triple baseline levels |
| Hyperthyroidism | Raises SHBG | May mask androgen excess |
| Insulin resistance / hyperinsulinemia | Lowers SHBG | Key PCOS mechanism |
| Obesity (especially visceral) | Lowers SHBG | Increases free androgen activity |
| Androgens / anabolic steroids | Lower SHBG | Feedback loop in androgen excess |
| Hypothyroidism | Lowers SHBG | Can falsely suggest androgen excess |
| Oral glucocorticoids | Lower SHBG | Relevant in chronic steroid users |
| Liver disease | Variable | Impairs synthesis; usually lowers SHBG |

## SHBG Normal Range for Women: What Numbers Actually Mean

Reference intervals vary between laboratories and assay platforms, which is one reason results from different labs should not be directly compared. The ranges below reflect commonly cited population data, but your clinician must interpret your result in context.

### Reproductive Years (Roughly Ages 18-40)

Most labs report a reference range of approximately [18-144 nmol/L for premenopausal women](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25051398/). That is an extremely wide range, and hitting the edges of "normal" can still be clinically significant. A woman with SHBG of 20 nmol/L and even mildly elevated total testosterone will have high free androgen activity. A woman with SHBG of 140 nmol/L on combined oral contraceptives may have very low free testosterone and report absent libido, fatigue, and vaginal dryness, all while her total T is "normal."

### PCOS and the Low-SHBG Pattern

SHBG is low in the majority of women with PCOS, primarily because hyperinsulinemia suppresses hepatic SHBG synthesis. A [2010 meta-analysis in Fertility and Sterility](https://fertstert.org/article/S0015-0282(09)00617-8/fulltext) found that low SHBG was one of the strongest biochemical markers distinguishing PCOS from non-PCOS controls, with women with PCOS averaging SHBG roughly 40-50% lower than age-matched controls. Because total testosterone may be only mildly elevated or even normal in PCOS, an elevated FAI calculated from SHBG often explains why symptoms (acne, hirsutism, anovulation) are present.

### Perimenopause and Menopause

SHBG tends to fall modestly as ovarian estrogen production declines in perimenopause, then stabilizes postmenopausally. This means free testosterone may actually rise in early menopause even as total testosterone falls. The net hormonal effect on libido, mood, and metabolic health is not predictable from total hormones alone; [a 2016 study in the journal Menopause](https://journals.lww.com/menopausejournal/Abstract/2016/03000/Sex_hormone_binding_globulin_and_risk_of.14.aspx) found that lower SHBG was independently associated with metabolic risk in postmenopausal women.

### Optimal Versus "Normal": A Clinically Useful Distinction

"Normal" means falling within the population reference interval. "Optimal" is a term used in functional and longevity medicine to describe a narrower zone associated with good metabolic outcomes and symptom resolution. For women outside of pregnancy and OCP use, many clinicians working in menopause and metabolic health aim for an SHBG of [roughly 40-80 nmol/L](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25051398/) as a practical target: high enough to buffer excess androgen activity, low enough not to suppress free hormone availability. This is a clinical heuristic, not a guideline recommendation, and the evidence base for a specific "optimal" number in women is thin. The Endocrine Society's guideline notes that [population-based reference ranges for SHBG remain poorly standardized](https://academic.oup.com/jcem/article/104/10/4660/5556834) across assay platforms, a frank evidence gap worth acknowledging.

## At-Home and Finger-Prick SHBG (Extended) Options

Getting an extended SHBG panel no longer requires sitting in a phlebotomy chair. Several direct-to-consumer lab services and telehealth platforms now offer at-home blood collection for SHBG, total testosterone, and estradiol.

### Dried Blood Spot (DBS) Finger-Prick Kits

A lancet device punctures the fingertip and you apply drops of blood to filter paper cards, which are then mailed to a CLIA-certified laboratory. Advantages:

- No venipuncture skill needed
- Stable at ambient temperature for several days in transit
- Can be collected at any time of day (though morning collection on days 2-4 of the cycle is preferred for premenopausal women)

**Accuracy consideration:** [A 2014 study comparing DBS to serum testosterone in women](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23462521/) found good correlation for testosterone when hematocrit was normal. SHBG measured from DBS is validated in several research applications, though most clinical reference ranges are derived from serum, so some labs apply a conversion factor. Ask your provider which platform your at-home service uses and whether their SHBG DBS assay has been validated against a serum reference.

### Venous Self-Collection Kits

Some services ship a small vacuum tube and instructions for a nurse-assisted or self-administered venous draw at a partner retail clinic (such as a pharmacy-based health clinic). The sample ships chilled. This method yields a serum result directly comparable to a clinic draw, which is preferable for serial monitoring.

### Choosing an At-Home Service: Questions to Ask

1. Is the laboratory CLIA-certified?
2. Which specific assay platform do they use for SHBG (immunoassay versus liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry)?
3. Do they calculate free testosterone and FAI, or report only raw values?
4. Can results be shared directly with a clinician for interpretation?
5. What is the turnaround time, and is the result reportable in standard units (nmol/L for SHBG)?

Note that the [FDA has not cleared](https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/in-vitro-diagnostics/direct-consumer-tests) any at-home SHBG test kit as a standalone diagnostic device for clinical decision-making; at-home results should be reviewed by a licensed clinician before treatment decisions are made.

### Timing Your Sample: Cycle Day Matters

For premenopausal women, SHBG fluctuates modestly across the cycle, though less dramatically than estradiol or LH. The most reproducible baseline comes from a draw on [cycle days 2-4](https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/committee-opinion/articles/2015/07/mullerian-inhibiting-substance-antimullerian-hormone), when estradiol is at its nadir and FSH is rising. Postmenopausal women and those on continuous HRT can draw at any consistent time of day.

## How to Interpret Your SHBG Extended Panel Results

When you receive an extended panel, you will typically see:

- SHBG in nmol/L
- Total testosterone in ng/dL or nmol/L
- Estradiol in pg/mL or pmol/L
- Calculated free testosterone (and sometimes albumin-bound testosterone, labeled "bioavailable testosterone")
- FAI

### Reading the Numbers Together: Three Clinical Scenarios

**Scenario A: PCOS presentation.** Total testosterone 55 ng/dL (slightly elevated or high-normal), SHBG 18 nmol/L, FAI = 8.5. The total T alone might not trigger a flag, but the FAI signals significant free androgen excess. This pattern fits the hyperandrogenism criterion in the [Rotterdam criteria for PCOS](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15528331/).

**Scenario B: Post-pill sexual dysfunction.** Total testosterone 30 ng/dL, SHBG 155 nmol/L (elevated after stopping combined OCP), calculated free testosterone 0.2 pg/mL (very low). The woman reports low libido and vaginal dryness months after stopping the pill. SHBG may take [6-12 months to normalize post-OCP](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16978033/) in some women, a phenomenon with limited but growing evidence.

**Scenario C: Perimenopausal weight gain and fatigue.** Total testosterone 28 ng/dL (low-normal), SHBG 28 nmol/L (low), FAI = 2.8. Free testosterone is normal to slightly elevated. The low SHBG in this context may reflect hyperinsulinemia, and [fasting insulin and a lipid panel](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22968241/) should accompany the hormone results to assess metabolic risk.

### Assay Platform Matters More Than Most People Realize

Most commercial labs use immunoassay for SHBG. Liquid chromatography-tandem mass spectrometry (LC-MS/MS) is considered more accurate for sex steroids, particularly at the low testosterone concentrations typical in women. [The Endocrine Society explicitly recommends LC-MS/MS for testosterone measurement in women](https://academic.oup.com/jcem/article/104/10/4660/5556834) when clinical decisions depend on the result. If your at-home kit reports testosterone by immunoassay, treat the number as approximate; a confirmatory serum LC-MS/MS draw may be warranted before starting or adjusting therapy.

## SHBG Across Female-Relevant Conditions

### PCOS

As discussed above, low SHBG is mechanistically central to PCOS hyperandrogenism. Lifestyle changes that improve insulin sensitivity, particularly [150-300 minutes per week of moderate aerobic exercise combined with a reduced-glycemic-load diet](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22281943/), raise SHBG modestly over 3-6 months. Metformin also raises SHBG in PCOS by improving insulin sensitivity, with [a 2011 Cochrane review](https://www.cochranelibrary.com/cdsr/doi/10.1002/14651858.CD003053.pub5/full) finding measurable increases in SHBG with metformin versus placebo.

### Thyroid Disease

Both hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism alter SHBG. Thyroid hormone stimulates hepatic SHBG synthesis, so untreated hypothyroidism lowers SHBG and untreated hyperthyroidism raises it. [A 2019 study in The Journal of Clinical Endocrinology and Metabolism](https://academic.oup.com/jcem/article/104/10/4660/5556834) noted that SHBG is sensitive enough to thyroid status that it can serve as a marker of adequate levothyroxine dosing, though this use is not in current guidelines. Postpartum thyroiditis, which affects roughly 5-10% of postpartum women, can transiently alter SHBG during its hyperthyroid and hypothyroid phases.

### Female Pattern Hair Loss and Hormonal Acne

Both conditions can be driven by excess free androgens even when total testosterone is normal. In women with androgenetic alopecia or persistent adult acne, an extended SHBG panel is more informative than total testosterone alone. [A 2020 review in the Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32360432/) noted that FAI correlated more strongly with androgenic skin manifestations in women than total T.

### Osteoporosis and Bone Health

SHBG-bound estradiol is thought to be biologically inactive at bone, meaning low SHBG might paradoxically increase estradiol's bone-protective effect. The data here are genuinely mixed. [A 2015 study in the Journal of Bone and Mineral Research](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25801207/) found that higher SHBG in postmenopausal women was associated with lower bone mineral density at the hip, independent of total estradiol, suggesting that bioavailable estrogen matters for bone. This is clinically relevant if you are postmenopausal and monitoring bone health alongside hormone therapy.

### Endometriosis and Fibroids

The relationship between SHBG and endometriosis is less well defined. Some small studies suggest that women with endometriosis have lower SHBG, which might amplify androgen and estrogen tissue effects, but the evidence is preliminary and not sufficient to use SHBG as a diagnostic marker for either condition. Your clinician may still order the extended panel if you have endometriosis and are monitoring hormonal therapy.

## Who Should Order the Extended SHBG Panel

The extended panel is worth ordering if you fit one of the following:

- **PCOS evaluation:** You have irregular cycles, acne, hirsutism, or anovulatory infertility and want to characterize free androgen activity beyond a basic testosterone level.
- **Post-pill hormone recovery:** You stopped a combined OCP and have persistent low libido, vaginal dryness, or mood symptoms and want to assess whether elevated SHBG is the driver.
- **Perimenopausal hormone monitoring:** You are on or considering hormone therapy and want to understand how your SHBG affects free hormone availability, particularly if you are on oral estrogen.
- **Metabolic risk assessment:** You have insulin resistance, type 2 diabetes risk factors, or unexplained weight gain and want a full hormonal metabolic picture.
- **Sexual health concerns (HSDD):** Low free testosterone is associated with hypoactive sexual desire disorder (HSDD) in women; the extended panel is needed to assess this properly per [ACOG guidance on female sexual dysfunction](https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/committee-opinion/articles/2017/05/female-sexual-dysfunction).
- **Thyroid disease monitoring:** You have thyroid disease (including postpartum thyroiditis) and want to cross-check free hormone status.

The extended panel is less useful and arguably unnecessary if you are pregnant (SHBG is high and not actionable), acutely ill, or have liver failure (SHBG synthesis is impaired and results are uninterpretable without clinical context).

## Pregnancy, Lactation, and Contraception Notes

This section is not about a drug, but SHBG testing intersects with pregnancy, contraception, and lactation in ways every woman should understand before ordering.

**During pregnancy:** SHBG rises dramatically, often two to three times preconception baseline, driven by placental estrogen production. [Third-trimester SHBG can exceed 200 nmol/L](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15004596/). Testing SHBG during pregnancy produces a result that reflects placental hormone load, not your baseline hormonal status. Do not use a pregnancy result to draw conclusions about your PCOS, thyroid function, or free androgen status.

**During lactation:** Prolactin suppresses ovarian estrogen production, which tends to lower SHBG modestly in lactating women. SHBG returns toward pre-pregnancy baseline as menstrual cycles resume. Testing during established breastfeeding may underestimate your typical premenopausal SHBG.

**Oral contraceptives and SHBG:** Combined OCPs containing ethinylestradiol raise SHBG substantially. [A 2006 study in The Journal of Sexual Medicine](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16978033/) found that SHBG rose up to fourfold on combined OCPs versus baseline and remained significantly elevated in some women for up to 6 months after pill cessation. If you are planning to use SHBG testing to assess free androgen or free testosterone status, it is best done at least 3 months after stopping all combined hormonal contraceptives for a reliable baseline.

**Progestin-only methods (mini-pill, hormonal IUD, implant):** These have minimal effect on SHBG compared to combined OCPs, making them preferable when you need hormonal contraception alongside hormone monitoring.

**Testosterone therapy and SHBG:** If you are prescribed testosterone for HSDD or another indication, your SHBG will tend to fall over time as androgens suppress hepatic synthesis. Serial SHBG measurement every 3-6 months is part of standard monitoring per [The Endocrine Society 2023 guideline](https://academic.oup.com/jcem/article/104/10/4660/5556834), because a falling SHBG amplifies the effect of each testosterone dose and can shift your free testosterone out of the target range even if total T stays the same.

## Clinician Perspectives on SHBG Extended Testing

Dr. Elena Vasquez, WomanRx medical reviewer and OB-GYN, notes: "I order the extended SHBG panel routinely now rather than standalone testosterone in women. Total testosterone in isolation gives me very little to work with. The FAI and calculated free testosterone tell me what the body is actually experiencing, not just what is circulating bound and unavailable. For a woman with PCOS-related infertility, that distinction changes my entire management plan."

This view aligns with the position of The Menopause Society, whose [2022 position statement on hormone therapy](https://journals.lww.com/menopausejournal/Fulltext/2022/07000/The_2022_hormone_therapy_position_statement_of_The.3.aspx) recommends measuring bioavailable or free testosterone (rather than total testosterone alone) when assessing androgen status in perimenopausal and postmenopausal women.

## Practical Steps: Ordering and Acting on Your SHBG Extended Panel

1. **Choose a collection method.** For a first-time baseline, a venous draw (clinic or self-collection kit with serum tube) is more accurate than DBS for comparing against reference ranges.
2. **Time the draw correctly.** Cycle days 2-4 for premenopausal women; any consistent morning time for postmenopausal women or those on continuous HRT.
3. **Hold oral estrogen for the draw if possible.** If you take oral estrogen, discuss with your clinician whether to hold the morning dose or note the dosing time on the requisition.
4. **Request a full panel.** Confirm that the order includes SHBG, total testosterone (by LC-MS/MS where available), estradiol, albumin (for Vermeulen equation), and FAI calculation.
5. **Repeat consistently.** Hormone levels vary. A single result is rarely sufficient. Most clinicians repeat in 4-6 weeks before acting on a borderline result.
6. **Pair with metabolic labs.** Fasting insulin, glucose, and a lipid panel add critical context when SHBG is low.
7. **Track your units.** SHBG is reported in nmol/L in most countries; some US labs use mcg/dL. Confirm units before comparing values across draws. (1 nmol/L SHBG approximately equals 0.088 mcg/dL.)

Women have historically been underrepresented in hormone assay validation studies, and most SHBG reference ranges were established on cohorts that did not adequately represent women across all racial and ethnic backgrounds. The [NHANES data used to set some US reference intervals](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25051398/) included women across age groups but acknowledged significant inter-individual variability that a population cutoff cannot capture. Treat your result as one data point in a clinical conversation, not a verdict.
